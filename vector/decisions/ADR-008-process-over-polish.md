# ADR-008: Process Over Polish

**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Status:** accepted
**Deciders:** Rob Jones

## Context

Design portfolios tend toward one of two extremes: all process (affinity maps, sticky notes, journey maps) with no visual craft, or all polish (Dribbble-ready screens) with no evidence of thinking. Hiring managers (PER-001) are frustrated by both: "Portfolios that show only polished final screens, no process, no decisions, no tradeoffs."

## Decision

Lead with process and thinking. Final UI lives on the homepage as video loops. Case studies document the research, decisions, and tradeoffs behind the work. Design decisions are structured as Problem/Decision/Why, not just before/after screenshots.

The case study is not a showcase of screens. It is an explanation of why those screens exist and what would have happened if different decisions were made.

## Consequences

- Case studies are text-heavy by design, which may lose visual-first reviewers
- The homepage video loops compensate: visual craft is demonstrated there, process is demonstrated in the case study
- Design decisions section gives hiring managers the "why" they are looking for (PER-001)
- The structure maps directly to interview questions: "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a stakeholder"

## Alternatives Considered

- **Polish-first (Dribbble style):** Looks impressive but gives hiring managers no signal about thinking or collaboration.
- **Process-only (sticky note heavy):** Shows research but doesn't prove the designer can ship refined UI.
- **Equal split:** Tried, but the homepage needed to lead with visual quality while the case study needed to lead with thinking. Splitting the job between two surfaces worked better than trying to do both in one.
